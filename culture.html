Culture

Get to know the E-Cell and the culture around here

Entrepreneurship Culture at IIT(BHU), Varanasi, is vibrant and it is very old. Many of our alumni are successful entrepreneurs. Also, E-Cell is there in IIT(BHU) to promote startup culture with the motto “Help will always be given to those who seek it”. Throughout the year, E-Cell, IIT(BHU), Varanasi, organizes startup events and at the same provides a platform for the budding startups to launch their product into the market.

TIIC - IIT(BHU) Incubation Centre TIIC - IIT(BHU) Incubation Centre

Technology Innovation and Incubation Centre (TIIC)-IIT(BHU) Varanasi is an incubation centre to bring the various schemes running in the institute, NCL-IIT(BHU) incubation Centre, RKVY-RAFTAAR Agri-Business Incubator, CISCO thingQbator, under one roof, for fostering entrepreneurship and nurturing tech start-ups. It manages and provides support for technology-based entrepreneurship and facilitates the transformation of research activity into entrepreneurial ventures.

The Rashtriya Krishi Vikas Yojna(RKVY), is an important scheme of the Government Of India, Ministry of Agriculture(MoA) and Farmers Welfare(FW), sanctioned to establish an RKVY-RAFTAAR Agri-Business Incubator(R-ABI) at IIT(BHU), Varanasi. It aims at strengthening infrastructure in agriculture and allied areas to promote agro-preneurship and agribusiness by financial support. Under this scheme, new agro-based business incubators will be established as well as existing agribusiness incubators strengthened as R-ABI with need-based infrastructure, equipment, and manpower.

"Cisco thingQbator" is a network of maker-spaces in partner Universities where students can learn about digital technologies in a hands-on environment, turn their ideas into working prototypes, and in the process, comes up with local solutions to local problems.
The name “thingQbator” is a combination of ‘Internet of Things’ and ‘Incubator’. thingQbator was started as an internal incubator of IoT ideas coming from Cisco’s engineer community. With the central belief that none of us is smarter than all of us, and the aim was to create maker spaces where IoT enthusiasts could learn more about digital technologies in a hands-on environment and turn their ideas into working prototypes.

Business Club

The Business Club is a de-facto place where the confluence of Economics, Consulting, Data Analytics, and Finance transpires. The Club promotes the learning and understanding of the same via group discussions, brainstorming sessions, case studies, debates, and stock simulations. Our vision is to collaborate with the consulting clubs of top B-schools, make premium knowledge platforms accessible, enrich skills through lectures from various speakers, and projects to hone the skills by testing them on real-life problems.
In January 2019, Satya Nadella, the CEO of Microsoft, appreciated the Club's work by saying "you are doing important work to shape what comes next for all of us. Good luck and thank you so much".
